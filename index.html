PHOTOGRAPHY

A photography course teaches camera basics, composition, lighting, and editing (like Photoshop/Lightroom) for various genres (portraits, wildlife, commercial) through certificate programs, diplomas, or degrees, with options from colleges (Bharati Vidyapeeth, MIT-WPU) and private institutes (Zee Institute, Starglazze, The Lens India) in Pune, offering practical skills, portfolio building, and career paths in creative fields. Courses range from short workshops to full degrees, catering to beginners to pros, focusing on both technical science and artistic expression.

Core Concepts

Light: The fundamental element, captured and focused by a lens.
Camera: The device that captures the light, from phone cameras to DSLRs.
Medium: How the image is stored—digital sensors (CMOS/CCD) or light-sensitive film.
Exposure: The amount of light hitting the sensor/film, controlled by aperture, shutter speed, and ISO.

||History & Evolution||

Origins:
The first permanent photo, "View from the Window at Le Gras," was taken by Joseph Nicéphore Niépce in 1826.
Early Processes:
Daguerreotype (1839), wet/dry plates, and the invention of the negative by Sir John Herschel.
Mass Market:
Kodak's "You press the button, we do the rest" cameras (1888) and the affordable Brownie (1900) made photography accessible.
Digital Revolution:
The 21st century shifted to digital sensors, with smartphones becoming ubiquitous cameras.
